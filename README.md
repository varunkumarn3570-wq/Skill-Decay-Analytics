# Skill-Decay-Analytics
End-to-End Workforce Skill  Decay Analytics Dashboard using SQL, Python, and Power BI
Workforce Skill Monitoring & Decay Analysis

Overview

Workforce Skill Monitoring & Decay Analysis is an end-to-end Data Analytics project designed to help organizations identify skill gaps, monitor workforce readiness, and prioritize employee upskilling initiatives.

As technologies continue to evolve rapidly, organizations face the challenge of ensuring that employee skills remain relevant. This project provides a data-driven approach to identify employees at risk of skill decay and supports proactive workforce development strategies.

---

Business Problem

Organizations often invest significant resources in employee training, yet many skills become underutilized or outdated over time. Without proper monitoring, skill decay can negatively impact productivity, innovation, and organizational growth.

This project helps answer critical business questions such as:

- Which employees require immediate upskilling?
- Which departments have the highest concentration of skill decay?
- How does training impact employee performance?
- Which skills are becoming less relevant over time?
- How can HR teams optimize training investments?

---

Project Objectives

- Identify employees with high skill decay scores.
- Classify employees into risk categories.
- Analyze relationships between training, performance, and skill utilization.
- Monitor skill usage trends across departments.
- Generate actionable recommendations for HR and management teams.

---

Key Insights

- 249 employees were identified as High Risk.
- Sales department showed the highest concentration of skill decay.
- Employees with lower risk levels demonstrated stronger performance outcomes.
- Skills with lower usage frequency were more likely to experience higher decay.
- Targeted retraining programs can significantly improve workforce readiness.

---

Business Recommendations

1. Prioritize retraining programs for High-Risk employees.
2. Focus learning initiatives on departments with higher skill decay levels.
3. Continuously monitor critical skills with declining utilization.
4. Align employee development plans with emerging technology trends.
5. Establish proactive workforce capability monitoring processes.
